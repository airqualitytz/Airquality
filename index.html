Odour Assessments for Planning Applications

AAE have extensive experience in odour assessments. Odour emissions are potentially generated from multiple sources including:

Commercial Kitchens

Odours from commercial kitchens, restaurants and fast food outlets can impact surrounding areas and require thorough assessments to ensure compliance and minimal nuisance.

Intensive Livestock Farming

Odour emissions from intensive livestock farming and slurry management are significant and need proper management to avoid adverse effects on nearby communities.

Abattoirs

Odours from abattoirs can be challenging to control. Proper assessments help in mitigating the impact on surrounding areas.

Wastewater and Sewage Treatment

Effective odour control in wastewater and sewage treatment plants is essential to prevent unpleasant smells affecting nearby areas.

Waste Management and Incineration

Odour emissions from waste management and incineration processes must be controlled to avoid impacts on the environment and nearby populations.

Composting Facilities

Proper odour management in composting facilities is crucial to prevent nuisances to the surrounding areas.

Industrial Processes

Various industrial processes can produce odours that require thorough assessments and control measures.

If your project is likely to cause an odour that will impact on its surroundings, or if your proposed development is near to any existing odour sources, an odour assessment may be required as part of your planning application.

Our consultants have developed longstanding relationships with local authorities and Environmental Health Officers and will happily liaise with them on your behalf to overcome any potential odour issues.

We aim to give your planning application the best chance of approval, first time.

Indoor Air Quality Plants & Their Importance

Spider Plant

Spider plants are known for removing pollutants like formaldehyde and xylene from the air.

Peace Lily

Peace lilies help in reducing mold spores and are great for improving air quality.

Boston Fern

Boston ferns are effective in removing pollutants like formaldehyde and are good humidifiers.

Snake Plant

Snake plants are excellent at removing toxins such as formaldehyde, xylene, and toluene from the air.
